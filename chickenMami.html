CHICKEN MAMI

Chicken Mami is a popular Filipino dish that is essentially a type of chicken noodle soup. It’s known for its flavorful broth, fresh egg noodles, and tender pieces of chicken123. The soup base is typically made from chicken bones that are sautéed and simmered for hours with carrots, celery, and other aromatics1. The dish is often garnished with vegetables such as napa cabbage, julienned carrots, and topped with hard-boiled eggs123. Additional flavor is added with the use of fish sauce and calamansi, a type of citrus fruit native to the Philippines123. Chicken Mami is considered a comfort food and is commonly enjoyed during colder seasons123. It can be served as a main course or a hearty snack123. The dish is versatile and can be customized with various toppings according to personal preference123. It’s not only delicious but also provides warmth and nourishment.

DIRECTIONS

1. Combine chicken broth and water in a cooking pot. Let boil[^1^][1].

2. Add salt and chicken breasts. Cover and boil between low to medium heat for 30 to 35 minutes[^1^][1].

3. Remove the chicken from the pot. Let it cool down. Shred the meat from the bone. Set aside[^1^][1].

4. Save the broth used for boiling the chicken[^1^][1].

5. Slice the carrot and Napa cabbage[^1^][1].

6. Boil the egg noodles for 3 minutes and drain the water[^1^][1].

7. Divide noodles equally in bowls. Add chicken meat, julienned carrots, shredded napa cabbage, and hard-boiled egg[^1^][1].

8. Pour hot broth. Garnish with fried garlic bits and chopped scallions[^1^][1]

INGREDIENTS

  • 1 lb. Chicken Breasts, bone-in

  • 1 piece Carrot, peeled

  • ½ piece Napa Cabbage

  • 1 bunch Scallions

  • 1 lb. Egg Noodles, fresh

  • ¼ cup Toasted Garlic

  • 4 pieces Hard Boiled Egg, sliced

  • 4 cups Chicken Broth

  • 4 cups Water

  • 2 teaspoons Salt
