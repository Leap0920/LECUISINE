ADOBONG PUSIT

Adobong Pusit, also known as Squid Adobo, is a popular Filipino dish that combines the country’s love for seafood and its iconic adobo cooking method. The squid is first marinated in soy sauce and vinegar, then sautéed with garlic, onions, and tomatoes, creating a rich and flavorful sauce. The dish is known for its dark color, which comes from the squid ink. It’s recommended to use small to medium-sized squids as they are more flavorful. The key to a tender Adobong Pusit is not to overcook the squid, preserving its tender texture. This dish is often served with warm rice and is a favorite in Filipino households.

DIRECTIONS

1. Combine squid, salt, soy sauce, and vinegar in a bowl. Mix well. Marinate for 12 minutes.

2. Heat oil in a cooking pot. Saute garlic until it starts to turn light brown. Add onion and tomato. Continue to saute until onion and tomato completely softens.

3. Pour the marinade liquid into the pot. Add ground black pepper. Let boil and then cook for 3 minute using low to medium heat. Make sure to cover the pot.

4. Add the squid. Let the sauce boil. Cover the pot and continue cooking for 3 minutes.

5. Add sugar and then remove the squid from the pot. Arrange in a clean bowl. Let the sauce cook until it reduces to half.

6. Pour the sauce all over the squid. Top with chopped parsley. Serve with warm rice.

INGREDIENTS

  • 1 kg Squid, cleaned and sliced

  • 2 tablespoons Soy Sauce

  • 5 tablespoons White Vinegar

  • 1 teaspoon Salt

  • 1 piece Onion, chopped

  • 1 piece Tomato, chopped

  • 5 cloves Garlic, crushed

  • 1/2 teaspoon Sugar

  • 3 tablespoons Cooking Oil
