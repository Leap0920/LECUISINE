LECHE PLAN

Leche Flan is a classic Filipino dessert that is enjoyed throughout the year, particularly during festive seasons and family gatherings. It’s a type of custard dessert made primarily from milk, sugar, and eggs, giving it a rich and creamy texture. The dessert is often enhanced with a hint of vanilla flavoring. Traditionally, it is prepared in an oval-shaped tin mold known as a llanera, which gives it its distinctive shape. Once cooked, it has a caramel topping that is created during the cooking process. Leche Flan is not only enjoyed on its own but is also a popular topping in halo-halo, a famous Filipino mixed dessert. The dessert’s origins can be traced back to the Spanish colonization period, which is why it shares similarities with the Spanish flan.

DIRECTIONS

1. In a saucepan, combine sugar and water over medium heat.
Allow the sugar to dissolve and caramelize until it turns golden brown. Swirl the pan occasionally to distribute the caramel evenly.
Once the caramel is ready, pour it into the bottom of the llanera (circular flan mold) or any heatproof container. Swirl the container to coat the bottom with caramel.

2. In a bowl, gently whisk the egg yolks.
Add the sweetened condensed milk and evaporated milk to the egg yolks, and whisk until well combined.
Add vanilla extract and continue to mix until the mixture is smooth.

3. To achieve a smoother consistency, strain the custard mixture through a fine-mesh sieve or cheesecloth to remove any lumps.

4. Carefully pour the custard mixture into the prepared mold with caramel, using a ladle or a funnel if needed.

5. Cover the mold with aluminum foil or its lid.
Steam the Leche Flan over medium heat for about 30-45 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.

6. Allow the Leche Flan to cool to room temperature before transferring it to the refrigerator.
Chill the Leche Flan for at least a few hours or overnight to let it set.

7. Once fully chilled, run a knife around the edges of the mold to loosen the Leche Flan.
Invert the mold onto a serving plate, allowing the caramel to flow over the custard.

INGREDIENTS

  • 1 cup of Granulated Sugar

  • 8 Egg Yolks

  • 1 can (14 ounces) Sweetened Condensed Milk

  • 1/4 cup of Water

  • 1 can (12 ounces) Evaporatetd Milk

  • 1 teaspoon Vanilla Extract
