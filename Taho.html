TAHO

Taho is a popular and delicious Filipino street food or snack that consists of sweet arnibal (caramelized sugar), soft and silky tofu, and sago pearls (similar to tapioca pearls). It's often sold by street vendors in the morning, calling out "Taho!" as they carry large containers strapped to a pole across their shoulders. Taho is typically served warm and is enjoyed by people of all ages.

DIRECTIONS

1. Boil water in a pot. Add the sago pearls and cook according to the package instructions or until they become soft and translucent.
Once cooked, drain and rinse the sago pearls under cold water to prevent sticking. Set aside.

2. In a separate saucepan, combine brown sugar and water.
Heat over medium heat, stirring continuously until the sugar dissolves and the mixture thickens into a syrup.
Optional: Add vanilla extract for flavor. Set aside to cool.

3. Place a spoonful of cooked sago pearls into serving cups or bowls.
Add cubes of soft tofu on top of the sago pearls.

4. Pour the prepared arnibal syrup over the tofu and sago pearls, allowing it to coat the entire mixture.

5. Taho is traditionally enjoyed warm, so serve it immediately after assembling.
Use a spoon to mix the components before eating, ensuring each bite has a balance of tofu, sago pearls, and sweet arnibal.

INGREDIENTS

  • 1 pack (about 12 ounces) soft or silken Tofu, cut into small cubes

  • 1 cup Brown Sugar

  • 1 cup small Sago Pearls

  • 1 cup of Water

  • 1/2 teaspoon Vanilla Extract
