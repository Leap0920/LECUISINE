SINIGANG NA BABOY

Sinigang na Baboy is a classic Filipino dish known for its savory and sour medley of flavors. This sour soup is traditionally made with pork belly or ribs, and a variety of vegetables such as okra, radish, and water spinach. The distinctive sour flavor comes from tamarind, which is the most common souring agent used, although other fruits like guava, green mango, and calamansi can also be used. Sinigang na Baboy is a comfort food in the Philippines, often enjoyed with a side of warm rice. It’s especially popular during rainy days, providing warmth and nourishment.

DIRECTIONS

1. Put water into a cooking pot, and then allow it to boil.

2. Add to it tomatoes and onions then cook for about 8 minutes.

3. Place the pork into the pot then simmer for about 25 minutes.

4. Add the taro. Cover and cook for 30 more minutes.

5. Add black pepper ground, fish sauce and Knorr sinigang sa Sampaloc mix then stir to combine.

INGREDIENTS

  • 1 kg Pork Belly, cut into 2-inch cubes

  • 2 tablespoons Fish Sauce

  • 1 Tamarind, juiced

  • 2 tablespoons Vegetable Oil

  • 4 cloves Garlic, minced

  • 1/2 large red onion, chopped

  • 2 cups Water

  • 1/2 Cup Gabi

  • 2 cups beef stock
