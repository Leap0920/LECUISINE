BIKO

Biko is a sweet rice cake from the Philippines. It is made of coconut milk, brown sugar, and sticky rice. It is usually topped with latik (either or both the coconut curds or the syrupy caramel-like variant).

DIRECTIONS

1. Wash the glutinous rice until the water runs clear.
Soak the rice in water for at least 30 minutes to 1 hour.

2. If using banana leaves, cut them into pieces to line the bottom of the pan.
Pass them over an open flame to make them more pliable.

3. Drain the soaked rice and steam it until it becomes fully cooked and sticky.
This usually takes about 20-30 minutes.

4. In a separate pan, heat coconut cream over medium heat until it separates into coconut oil and latik (toasted coconut milk solids).
This can take some time, so be patient. Stir occasionally to prevent burning.

5. In a saucepan, combine coconut milk, brown sugar, water, and salt.
Cook over medium heat, stirring continuously, until the mixture thickens into a syrup-like consistency.

6. Once the rice is cooked, transfer it to a large bowl.
Pour the coconut syrup over the rice and mix well until the rice is evenly coated.

7. If using banana leaves, line a baking pan with them.
Transfer the rice mixture into the pan and flatten the surface with a spatula.
Bake in a preheated oven at 350°F (175°C) for about 20-30 minutes or until the top is golden brown.

8. If you made latik, you can spread it over the top of the biko for added flavor.

9. Allow the biko to cool before slicing it into squares or diamonds.
Serve and enjoy!

INGREDIENTS

  • 2 cups of Glutinous Rice

  • 2 cups of Coconut Milk

  • 2 cups of Brown Sugar

  • 1/2 cup of Water

  • 1/2 teaspoon Salt

  • (optional, for lining the pan) Banana Leaves

  • (optional, for topping) Latik
