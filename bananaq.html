BANANA CUE

Banana Cue is a beloved Filipino street food delicacy that transforms ordinary bananas into a delightful sweet treat. Ripe bananas are peeled and cut into manageable halves, and then generously coated in rich, caramelized brown sugar. The magic happens in a sizzling pan of hot vegetable oil, where the sugar-coated bananas are carefully fried to perfection. The result is a crispy, golden-brown exterior that gives way to a soft and sweet interior, creating a delightful contrast in texture. The caramelization process imparts a deep, toasty flavor to the bananas, making Banana Cue a flavorful and indulgent snack. Often served on sticks for convenient street-side enjoyment, this popular Filipino dessert captures the essence of comfort and simplicity, making it a cherished part of local culinary culture.

DIRECTIONS

1. Peel the bananas and cut them in half, crosswise.

2. Heat the vegetable oil in a pan over medium heat.

3. Roll each banana piece in brown sugar, ensuring they are well coated.

4. Carefully place the sugar-coated bananas in the hot oil, turning occasionally to caramelize all sides.

5. Fry until the bananas are golden brown and the sugar has caramelized, forming a crispy coating.

6. Using a slotted spoon, remove the banana cue from the pan and let excess oil drip off.

7. Place the banana cue on a plate or skewers for serving.

8. Allow them to cool for a few minutes before serving.

INGREDIENTS

  • 6 ripe bananas

  • 1 cup brown sugar

  • 1 cup vegetable oil
