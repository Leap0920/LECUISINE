AFRITADA

Filipino Afritada is a savory stew known for its rich and hearty flavors. Typically made with tender chunks of meat, commonly chicken or pork, it is simmered in a tomato-based sauce infused with garlic, onions, and bell peppers. Potatoes and carrots are often added for a delightful balance, creating a comforting dish that embodies the vibrant and delicious nature of Filipino cuisine.

DIRECTIONS

1. Heat 2 to 3 tablespoons of oil in a large pot or pan.

2. Sauté minced garlic until golden, then add chopped onions.
Cook until onions are translucent.

3. Add the chicken or pork chunks, and brown them on all sides.

4. Pour in the tomato sauce and let it simmer for a few minutes.

5. Season with fish sauce or soy sauce according to your taste.
Add ground pepper and bay leaves for additional flavor.

6. Pour enough water to cover the meat and bring it to a boil.
Reduce heat to low, cover, and let it simmer until the meat is tender.

7. Once the meat is almost tender, add potatoes and carrots.
Simmer until the vegetables are cooked through.

8. Add the sliced bell peppers and cook for an additional 5-7 minutes until they are tender-crisp.

9. Taste and adjust the seasoning as needed.
You can add more fish sauce, salt, or pepper to achieve your desired flavor.

10. Once everything is cooked to perfection, remove the bay leaves and serve the Afritada hot.
It's often enjoyed with steamed rice.

INGREDIENTS

  • 1 to 1.5 pounds of chicken or Pork

  • 2 to 3 medium-sized peeled and sliced Potatoes

  • 2 to 3 medium-sized peeled and sliced Carrots

  • One 8-ounce can or more of Tomato Sauce

  • 1 to 2 sliced Bell Peppers

  • 1 large finely chopped Onion

  • 4 to 6 cloves, minced Garlic

  • 2 to 3 tablespoons Fish Sauce

  • 1 teaspoon, either whole or ground Peppercorns

  • 2 to 3 leaves of Bay Leaves

  • 2 to 3 tablespoon of Oil
