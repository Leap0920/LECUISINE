PALITAW

Palitaw is a traditional Filipino rice cake that is chewy, flat, and typically coated with grated coconut and sugar. It's a popular snack or dessert often enjoyed during special occasions or as a merienda (snack time).

DIRECTIONS

1. In a mixing bowl, combine glutinous rice flour, salt, and 3/4 cup of water.
Mix well until it forms a smooth and slightly sticky dough. Adjust the consistency by adding more water or glutinous rice flour as needed.

2. Take a small amount of dough and roll it into a ball. Flatten the ball into a disc shape, about 2 inches in diameter.
You can also make them smaller or larger based on your preference.

3. Bring a pot of water to a boil.
Drop the flattened dough into the boiling water. The Palitaw is cooked once it floats to the surface, which usually takes about 1-2 minutes.
Using a slotted spoon, carefully remove the cooked Palitaw and let them drain excess water.

4. Roll the cooked Palitaw in grated coconut until they are well coated.
Sprinkle sugar on top of the grated coconut-coated Palitaw. You can adjust the amount of sugar according to your sweetness preference.

5. Palitaw is best served while it's still warm. Arrange them on a serving plate and enjoy this delightful Filipino snack.

INGREDIENTS

  • 2 cups Glutinous Rice Flour

  • 1/4 teaspoon Salt

  • 1/2 cup Water for dipping the fingers

  • 3/4 cup of Water approximately, adjust as needed

  • White Sugar for coating

  • Grated Coconut for coating
