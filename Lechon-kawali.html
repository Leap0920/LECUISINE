LECHON-KAWALI

Lechon Kawali is a popular Filipino dish known for its crispy and flavorful deep-fried pork belly. The preparation involves boiling the pork until tender, then air-drying or refrigerating it to achieve a crispy skin when fried. The result is succulent and juicy meat on the inside with a crunchy and golden-brown exterior. It is commonly served with liver sauce or vinegar dipping sauce, adding a delightful contrast of textures and flavors to this classic Filipino delicacy.

DIRECTIONS

1. In a large pot, place the pork belly slices or slab.
Add enough water to cover the meat. Season with salt and pepper.
Bring to a boil and simmer until the pork is fork-tender (about 45 minutes to 1 hour).

2. Remove the boiled pork from the pot and let it air-dry or refrigerate for several hours or overnight.
This helps in achieving a crispy skin when fried.

3. Mix vinegar, minced garlic, salt, and pepper in a bowl. Set aside.

4. Heat enough cooking oil in a deep pan or wok for frying.
Once the oil is hot, carefully add the dried and cooled pork belly. Fry until the skin becomes crispy and golden brown, and the meat is fully cooked.

5. Remove the crispy pork from the oil and let it drain on paper towels.
Allow it to rest for a few minutes, then slice into serving pieces.

6. Arrange the Lechon Kawali on a serving plate and serve with the prepared dipping sauce.

INGREDIENTS

  • 1 kg Pork Belly

  • 4 to 6 cups of Water for boiling

  • 1 to 2 tablespoon of Salt

  • 1 to 2 tablespoon of Pepper

  • 4 to 6 cups of Cooking Oil for deep frying

  • 1/2 cup Vinegar

  • 3 cloves minced Garlic
