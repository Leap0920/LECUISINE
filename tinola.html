TINOLANG MANOK

Tinolang manok, which literally means “boiled chicken”, is a soup-based dish that consists of chicken pieces, green papaya or other vegetables, and leafy greens cooked in a broth flavored with garlic, salt, pepper, oregano, and bay leaves. It is usually served with steamed rice and seasoned with fish sauce, calamansi juice, or vinegar. Tinolang manok is considered a comfort food in the Philippines, as it can warm you up and nourish you with its rich flavor and ingredients. It is also a popular dish for special occasions, such as fiestas, birthdays, and holidays.

DIRECTIONS

1. Heat the vegetable oil in a pan, then sauté the garlic, onion, and ginger.

2. Add the chicken to the pan and cook until the color turns light brown.

3. Pour the water into the pan and bring it to a boil.

4. Add the green papaya to the pan and simmer for about 5 minutes.

5. Add the fish sauce and continue to simmer until the chicken and green papaya are tender.

6. Add the spinach or chili leaves and cook for an additional minute.

INGREDIENTS

  • 2 lbs. Chicken, cut into serving pieces

  • 1 piece Green Papaya, cut into wedges

  • 1 tbsp. Vegetable Oil

  • 4 cloves Garlic, minced

  • 1 large Onion, chopped

  • 1 thumb-sized Ginger, sliced

  • 2 cups Spinach

  • 1L Water

  • 2 tbsp. Fish Sauce
