POLVORON

Polvoron is a Filipino sweet delicacy that is characterized by its crumbly and powdery texture. It is made from a mixture of toasted flour, powdered milk, sugar, and butter, then shaped into round or oval molds. Polvoron is a popular snack or pasalubong (gift) in the Philippines.

DIRECTIONS

1. In a dry pan over low to medium heat, toast the all-purpose flour until it turns light brown.
Stir constantly to ensure even toasting. This step usually takes around 15-20 minutes. Let it cool.

2. In a large bowl, combine the toasted flour, powdered milk, and sugar. Mix the dry ingredients thoroughly.

3. Pour the melted unsalted butter into the dry ingredients. Mix well until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
Ensure that the butter is evenly distributed.

4. Use a polvoron mold or shape the mixture into round or oval molds by pressing it firmly into the mold.
If you don't have a mold, you can shape them by hand. The goal is to create compact, dense pieces.

5. Wrap each polvoron individually in wax paper or colored cellophane. Twist the ends to secure the wrap.

6. Place the wrapped polvoron in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes to allow them to set.

7. Once the polvoron has set, they are ready to be enjoyed. They are delicate and crumbly, so handle them gently

INGREDIENTS

  • 2 cups all-pupose Flour

  • 1 cup Powdered Milk

  • 1 cup unsalted Butter melted

  • 1 cup Sugar

  • Polvoron Mold (or you can shape them by hand)

  • Wax Paper

  • OR Colored Cellophane for wrapping
