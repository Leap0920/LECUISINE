BEEF MECHADO

Beef Mechado, also known as Mechadong Baka, is a traditional Filipino stew. It is made with tender chunks of beef marinated in soy sauce and calamansi juice, then sautéed with garlic, onions, and tomatoes. The dish is slow-cooked in a tomato-based sauce and often includes potatoes, bell peppers, and bay leaves. The original recipe involved a process called larding, where strips of pork back fat were woven through the beef to enhance its flavor. Nowadays, this method isn’t commonly used, but the dish remains a hearty, savory meal that pairs perfectly with warm rice. It’s typically prepared during weekends or special occasions due to its longer cooking time.

DIRECTIONS

1. Marinate the beef in soy sauce and lemon juice for at least 30 minutes.

2. Heat the vegetable oil in a pan, then sauté the garlic and onion.

3. Add the marinated beef to the pan, including the marinade, and continue to cook until the beef browns.

4. Add the tomato sauce and beef stock to the pan.

5. Cover the pan and simmer the mixture on low heat for about 90 minutes, or until the beef is tender. Add water as needed during this time.

6. Once the beef is tender, you can add any additional vegetables you like, such as potatoes or bell peppers, and cook until they are tender.

INGREDIENTS

  • 1 kg Beef Chuck, cut into 2-inch cubes

  • 2 tablespoons Soy Sauce

  • 1 Lemon, juiced

  • 2 tablespoons Vegetable Oil

  • 4 cloves Garlic, minced

  • 1/2 large red onion, chopped

  • 2 cups Tomato sauce

  • 1/2 Cup Soy Sauce

  • 2 cups beef stock
