MENUDO

Menudo, also known as Ginamay or Ginagmay, is a traditional Filipino stew. It’s typically made with diced pork and pork liver, cooked in a sweet tomato sauce. The dish is often accompanied by hearty potatoes, carrots, crunchy chickpeas, and green bell peppers. Some regional variations may include raisins, bell peppers, garbanzo beans, and sausages. Unlike the Mexican dish of the same name, Filipino Menudo does not use tripe or red chili sauce. This comforting stew is a staple at important events in the Philippines and is usually served with steamed rice

DIRECTIONS

1. Combine pork, soy sauce, and lemon in a bowl. Marinate for at least 1 hour.

2. Heat oil in a pan. Saute garlic and onion.

3. Add the marinated pork to the pan and cook it for five (5) to seven (7) minutes.

4. Add tomato sauce and water.

5. Let it boil and simmer for 30 minutes or until the pork is tender.

6. Add the liver and cook for 5 minutes.

7. Add potatoes, carrots, then season with salt, and pepper.

INGREDIENTS

  • 2 lbs. Pork

  • 1/4 lb. Pig Liver

  • 1 cup Potatoes, diced

  • 1 piece Carrot, cubed

  • 1/2 cup Soy Sauce

  • 1/2 piece Lemon

  • 1 piece Onion, chopped

  • 3 cloves Garlic, minced

  • 1 teaspoon Sugar

  • 3/4 cup Tomato Sauce

  • 1 cup Water

  • 4 pieces Hotdogs, sliced diagonally

  • 2 tablespoons Cooking Oil
