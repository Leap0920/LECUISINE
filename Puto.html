PUTO

Puto is a traditional Filipino steamed rice cake that is soft, fluffy, and slightly sweet. It is a popular snack or merienda and is often served during special occasions and celebrations. Puto can come in various colors and flavors, and it is typically enjoyed with savory or sweet accompaniments.

DIRECTIONS

1. In a large mixing bowl, combine rice flour, all-purpose flour, baking powder, and sugar. Mix well.

2. Pour in water and coconut milk into the dry ingredients. Mix until the batter is smooth and well combined.
Add vanilla extract if desired for extra flavor.

3. Preheat your steamer. If you don't have a steamer, you can use a large pot with a steaming rack or a makeshift steamer setup.

4. Lightly grease your puto molds or small individual cups with cooking oil or butter.
Pour the batter into the molds, filling each one about 2/3 full.

5. Arrange the filled molds in the steamer, leaving some space between them to allow for expansion.
Steam the puto over medium heat for about 15-20 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.

6. While the puto is still hot, you can add toppings like grated cheese or salted egg slices on top.

7. Allow the puto to cool slightly before removing them from the molds.
Serve the puto warm or at room temperature. They are delicious on their own or paired with savory dishes like dinuguan (pork blood stew).

INGREDIENTS

  • 2 cups Rice Flour

  • 1 cup All-Purpose Flour

  • 2 1/4 teaspoon Baking Powder

  • 1 1/2 cups Water

  • 1 1/2 cups Coconut Milk

  • 1 teaspoon Vanilla Extract(optional)

  • 1 1/2 cups Sugar

  • Grated Cheese OR

  • Salted Eggslice for toppings
