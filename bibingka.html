BANANA CUE

Bibingka is a beloved Filipino delicacy, celebrated for its unique blend of flavors and delightful texture. This traditional rice cake, often enjoyed during festive occasions and holidays, is made with rice flour, coconut milk, and sugar, creating a luscious batter that is both sweet and savory. The addition of melted butter and eggs contributes to its moist and tender consistency, while the use of banana leaves for lining imparts a distinct aroma. Topped with a generous sprinkling of grated coconut and sesame seeds, bibingka boasts a rich and satisfying taste, capturing the essence of Filipino culinary traditions.

DIRECTIONS

1. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). Grease a baking pan and line it with banana leaves.

2. In a large bowl, combine the rice flour, sugar, and baking powder.

3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the eggs and then add the melted butter and coconut milk. Mix well.

4. Gradually add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients, stirring until you have a smooth batter.

5. Pour the batter into the prepared baking pan and smooth the top with a spatula.

6. Sprinkle the grated coconut and sesame seeds on top of the batter.

7. Bake in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes or until the top is golden brown and a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.

8. Allow the bibingka to cool for a few minutes before slicing and serving. Enjoy your delicious Filipino bibingka!

INGREDIENTS

  • 2 cups Rice Flour

  • 1 cup Coconut Milk

  • 1 cup Sugar

  • 1/2 cup Butter, melted

  • 1 teaspoon Baking Powder

  • 3 Eggs

  • 1/2 cup Grated Coconut

  • 1/4 cup Sesame Seeds

  • Banana leaves for lining
